## Feature Comparison: Where Each Platform Shines

### Invoice Data Extraction Accuracy

Both platforms use AI and machine learning to extract key fields from invoices — vendor name, invoice number, date, line items, totals, tax amounts, and more.

**Nanonets** uses deep learning models that can be trained on your specific invoice layouts. While this can achieve high accuracy for standardized invoice formats, it comes with a trade-off: you need to invest time annotating at least 50 sample documents before the model reaches peak performance. For businesses that receive invoices from dozens of different vendors in different formats, this training overhead multiplies quickly.

**InvoiceToData** takes a different approach. Its [invoice data extraction engine](https://invoicetodata.com/) [INTERNAL LINK] is pre-trained to handle a wide variety of invoice layouts right out of the box — no annotation, no training data, no waiting. Upload an invoice, and you get structured data back immediately. This is particularly valuable for SMBs that deal with invoices from many different suppliers and don't have the engineering resources to train custom models.

### Ease of Use and Onboarding

One of the biggest differentiators between these two platforms is the time it takes to go from sign-up to extracting real data.

With **InvoiceToData**, the onboarding experience is designed for non-technical users. You can sign up, upload your first invoice, and receive extracted data within minutes. There's no model configuration, no training pipeline, and no need to involve your engineering team. The interface is clean and focused on one thing: turning invoices into structured data.

**Nanonets** offers a broader document processing platform, which means the interface is more complex. Setting up invoice extraction may require defining extraction fields, uploading training samples, and configuring validation rules. Several reviewers on Capterra and G2 have noted that integration setup alone can take more than a week, depending on the complexity of your ERP system.

### API and Developer Experience

For teams that want to integrate invoice extraction into their existing workflows programmatically, both platforms offer RESTful APIs.

**InvoiceToData's API** [INTERNAL LINK to API docs] is focused and well-documented. Because the platform is laser-focused on invoice processing, the API is straightforward: send an invoice (PDF, image, or scanned document), receive structured JSON data back. The simplicity makes it easy to integrate with accounting software, ERPs, or custom workflows.

**Nanonets' API** is more feature-rich but also more complex, reflecting its broader document processing capabilities. You'll need to manage model IDs, handle training endpoints, and configure extraction templates before you can start processing invoices at scale.

### Output and Export Options

**InvoiceToData** specializes in converting invoices into immediately usable formats. You can export extracted data directly to Excel spreadsheets, CSV files, or structured JSON — making it the ideal choice if you regularly need to [convert invoices to Excel](https://invoicetodata.com/) [INTERNAL LINK] or push data into accounting software.

**Nanonets** offers JSON and CSV exports, along with integrations to push data into third-party applications. However, some of the more advanced export and workflow automation features are locked behind enterprise-tier pricing.

## Pricing: Transparent vs Enterprise-Focused

Pricing is one of the areas where InvoiceToData and Nanonets diverge most significantly.

**InvoiceToData** offers transparent, publicly listed pricing with a pay-per-invoice model. This means you know exactly what you'll pay before you start, with no surprise costs as you scale. There's a free tier available so you can test the platform with real invoices before committing.

**Nanonets** provides a free tier covering the first 100 invoices, but after that, pricing is available "on request." Multiple review sites (including Capterra and G2) note that Nanonets pricing can be 37% to 50% higher than some competitors, and advanced features like workflow automation and custom integrations require enterprise-tier plans. For smaller businesses, this pricing opacity can be frustrating — you may not know the true cost until you're deep into a sales conversation.

**Bottom line:** If predictable, transparent pricing matters to your business, InvoiceToData has the edge. If you're a large enterprise with the budget for custom pricing negotiations, Nanonets may offer more flexibility.

## Handling International Invoices

A real-world consideration that often gets overlooked: how well does each tool handle international invoices?

**Nanonets** users have reported challenges with international documents. Because the platform is global, differences in date formats (DD/MM/YYYY vs MM/DD/YYYY), decimal separators (commas vs periods), and currency symbols can cause extraction errors that require manual review and correction.

**InvoiceToData** is designed to handle diverse invoice formats from the start, with built-in intelligence for recognizing regional formatting conventions — reducing the need for manual corrections when processing invoices from international suppliers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is InvoiceToData better than Nanonets for small businesses?

For most small businesses, yes. InvoiceToData requires no model training, offers transparent pricing, and is designed for non-technical users. Nanonets' strengths — custom model training and enterprise features — are more relevant to larger organizations with dedicated engineering teams.

### Can InvoiceToData handle the same invoice formats as Nanonets?

InvoiceToData's pre-trained AI handles a wide variety of invoice formats out of the box, including PDFs, scanned documents, and images. While Nanonets allows custom model training for unusual formats, InvoiceToData's zero-training approach covers the vast majority of real-world invoice layouts without any setup.

### Which tool has better API documentation?

Both platforms offer RESTful APIs. InvoiceToData's API is more focused and streamlined for invoice-specific use cases, making it faster to integrate. Nanonets' API is broader but more complex due to its multi-document-type support.

### Do I need technical skills to use InvoiceToData?

No. InvoiceToData is designed for non-technical users. You can upload invoices through the web interface or set up simple API integrations with minimal coding. Nanonets generally requires more technical expertise, particularly for model training and custom configurations.

### How accurate is InvoiceToData compared to Nanonets?

Both platforms offer high accuracy for standard invoices. InvoiceToData achieves strong out-of-the-box accuracy without any training. Nanonets can achieve comparable accuracy but typically requires training on your specific document types first — and even then, Nanonets users have noted that accuracy can be lower than manual processing in some cases.
